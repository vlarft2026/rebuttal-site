Metric definitions

Why we report Delta MSE and AVC

These metrics target dynamic consistency rather than only static image quality.

Delta MSE

Measures whether the predicted frame-to-frame change matches the ground-truth visual transition.

Δ o ^ t = o ^ t + 1 - o ^ t Δ o t = o t + 1 - o t Delta MSE = 1 T ∑t ∥ Δ o t - Δ o ^ t ∥ 2

Lower values indicate that the model more accurately captures the magnitude and direction of action-induced motion.

Symbols: ot is the ground-truth observation at step t, ôt is the predicted observation, Δot = ot+1 - ot is the frame difference, and T is the rollout horizon.

Action-Visual Correlation (AVC)

Measures the correlation between action magnitude and the magnitude of predicted visual change.

AVC = ∑ t ( ∥ a t ∥ - a ¯ ) ( ∥ Δ o ^ t ∥ - v ¯ ) ∑t ( ∥ a t ∥ - a ¯ ) 2 · ∑t ( ∥ Δ o ^ t ∥ - v ¯ ) 2

Higher AVC means stronger alignment between the commanded action and the predicted visual response, reducing the chance of producing action-agnostic smooth videos.

Symbols: at is the action at step t, ||at|| is its magnitude, ||Δôt|| is the magnitude of predicted visual change, and ā and v̄ denote the temporal means of these two quantities.
